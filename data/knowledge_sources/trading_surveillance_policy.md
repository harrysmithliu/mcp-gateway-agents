# Trading Surveillance Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines the minimum internal controls for monitoring suspicious trading activity across monitored accounts, wallets, and desks.

## Trigger Conditions
Analysts should review trading activity when one or more of the following conditions are present:
- rapid exposure growth within 24 hours
- repeated alert recurrence across the same account
- abnormal concentration in a single asset or trading pair
- unusual order volume relative to recent account behavior

## Analyst Actions
When a trigger condition is observed, the analyst should:
1. review recent orders and recent trades
2. compare exposure changes against the account risk profile
3. retrieve supporting evidence from internal knowledge and audit systems
4. escalate to a risk operator when the activity cannot be explained by normal strategy behavior

## Escalation Guidance
Escalation is required when suspicious trading activity is paired with high risk score, repeated alerts, or unresolved prior review findings.

## Recordkeeping
All investigation steps, evidence references, and escalation decisions must be captured in the audit trail.
